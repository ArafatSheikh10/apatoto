Melbourne Star Observation Wheel

Australia's most recognizable attractions, located in the Docklands precinct of Melbourne, Victoria. Standing 120 meters (394 feet) tall, it offered spectacular 360-degree panoramic views of Melbourne's skyline, Port Phillip Bay, and surrounding landmarks.

About Melbourne Star Observation Wheel

The Melbourne Star was designed as a world-class observation wheel, similar to the famous London Eye. Visitors enjoyed a 30-minute journey in climate-controlled cabins while taking in breathtaking views of Melbourne and its surroundings. The attraction was considered one of the largest observation wheels in the Southern Hemisphere.

The wheel became famous for its unique seven-spoke design, inspired by the seven-pointed star featured on the Australian flag. Although it closed in 2021, plans have been announced to reopen the attraction following extensive renovations.
